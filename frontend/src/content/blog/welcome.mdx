## The Mirror Question

Ever wanted an answer to the question above? Go back to the mirror, look into the reflection of your own eyes, and ask yourself — *"Are you the real one who you think you are?"*

In that moment, your mind may start to point out your flaws more than the things you've accomplished and the good you've done for your friends, family and society.

Based on my experience, I feel that finding yourself doesn't end at a particular age, nor is it a destination to reach.

<Callout type="tip" title="What actually matters">
  It's all about seeing yourself in the things you do, finding purpose, and adding value to the lives of others. You naturally attract people who share your energy.
</Callout>

Expecting nothing in return for the good you do is a remarkable quality everyone should cultivate. Because whenever you act selflessly, you open yourself to learning more and becoming willing to take risks.

<Divider />

## The Path Defines You

It's usual to have self doubt, obsession over something and being busy chasing our dreams. But the **process and the path you choose** to make your progress reaching there will define you and reflect who you actually are!

People like you and me usually come and go, but if we make efforts being a **remarkable time stamp in others' calendar**, collating these time stamps — that perspective, those kind words — will embrace your presence with them in your absence.

<Callout type="info" title="Something I've noticed">
  The ones who grow the fastest aren't the ones who work the hardest. They're the ones who are honest with themselves about who they currently are — and who they want to become.
</Callout>

## Life is a Rainbow

Life is nothing short of a rainbow, full of colours. We usually see just a thin white light and it's boring, right?
